# First Users Outreach Plan — Example Output
*Generated: 2026-06-14 | Target: first 10 users | Product: UpdateDraft*

## The ideal first user

**Who they are:** Seed-stage founder (not pre-seed, not Series A), 15-50 investors,
sends weekly or bi-weekly updates, has Stripe and Mercury set up, is technically
comfortable but not a developer.

**Why them first:** They have the problem acutely. They are likely in my network.
They will give honest feedback because they are peers, not customers they want to impress.

**What to watch for:** Do they go back and manually verify the numbers before sending?
Do they mention the source tags? Do they send it the week after the first time?

---

## Target user types (prioritized)

1. **Founders in my direct network who have complained about investor updates** — highest trust, will be honest
2. **Referrals from interview participants** — already know about the problem from the research process
3. **Founders in the YC/startup Slack groups where investor updates come up** — warm community context
4. **Second-degree founder connections** — slightly colder, but referral from a mutual makes it warm

---

## Outreach messages

>
> Would you be up for a 20-minute screen share this weekend? I'll set it up, you
> just bring your Sunday update workflow.
>
> — Sofia

## The onboarding ask

When someone agrees to try it:

"I'd love to do a 20-minute screen share while you run UpdateDraft on your actual
accounts. Here is exactly what I want you to do: connect your Stripe and Mercury,
let it pull your data, and then tell me if you would send those numbers to your
investors without going back to verify them in Stripe.

I'll be on the call but I'll mostly watch. I'm not going to help unless something
breaks. I want to see what happens when someone uses it for the first time without
guidance."

---

## Feedback capture

**Format:** Live screen share (20 min) + 3 questions after

**What to observe:**
- Do they read the source tags? Do they hover over them?
- Do they open Stripe or Mercury to verify? (the key signal)
- Where do they slow down or look uncertain?
- What do they say unprompted?

**Three questions to ask every user:**
1. "Would you send these numbers to your investors without going back to verify in Stripe? What would make you more or less likely to do that?"
2. "What happened that you didn't expect?"
3. "If you used this for the next month, what's the most likely reason you'd stop?"

**The single most important thing to learn from users 1-10:**
Whether even one person sends the auto-gathered numbers to investors without manually
verifying — and what specifically made them comfortable enough to do so.
