# Customer Interview Transcript — Interview #7

*Interviewee: Second-time founder, seed-stage (~18 employees)*
*Date: 2026-06-14*
*Format: 30-minute call*
*Background: Previous company raised Series B, acquired. Has sent 200+ investor updates.*

---

**Me:** You've sent a lot of investor updates at this point. What has changed from your first company to this one?

**Them:** The work has gotten faster because I know exactly what investors want. But the data gathering hasn't gotten faster — that part is always the same amount of work.

**Me:** What do you mean by "investors want the same things"?

**Them:** MRR, cash runway, top 3 wins, top 2 risks, what I need help with. That's it. I've been sending that format since 2019. My investors actually asked me not to change it.

**Me:** [Format stability confirmed again — this is now the fourth or fifth mention of this pattern.]

**Me:** Tell me what the data gathering looks like for you now.

**Them:** I do it Friday morning, not Sunday. I learned to not do it Sunday — it ruins the weekend. I go to Stripe, Mercury, and our analytics tool. Takes about 45 minutes. I've gotten pretty efficient at it.

**Me:** 45 minutes is still a lot.

**Them:** Yeah. And the annoying thing is it's not 45 minutes of hard thinking — it's 45 minutes of mechanical work. Go here, copy this, go there, copy that. That's the part that kills me. I'm not learning anything by doing it.

**Me:** Have you tried to automate it this time around, given your experience?

**Them:** I looked at a few tools. None of them did quite what I wanted. The BI tools are overkill — I don't need a dashboard, I need a document. The investor update tools I found basically write the update for me, which I don't want. I want to write it myself. I just want the data pre-loaded.

**Me:** [Sharp distinction: data pre-loaded vs. content generated. He doesn't want the writing automated.]

**Me:** That's an interesting distinction. Say more.

**Them:** The writing is where I think. It's where I figure out what's actually happening with the business. If a tool writes it for me, I lose that. But if I could open a document that already had my numbers in it, and then I just... wrote around them? That would be great.

**Me:** What would make you trust the numbers in that document?

**Them:** At this point I'd just want to see the source. I've been doing this long enough that I can spot a wrong number from a mile away. As long as I can see where it came from and when it was pulled, I'm fine. I'm not going to audit every number every week.

**Me:** Would you use it even if it got a number slightly wrong sometimes?

**Them:** Define "slightly." If it was off by a dollar, yes. If it was off by 5% in a way that changed the narrative, no. That's the kind of thing that damages investor relationships. But honestly, if the source is shown, I can catch that myself before I send.

**Me:** What would you pay for a tool that pre-loaded your data and showed the sources?

**Them:** I'd pay $150/month easily. Maybe $200. If I could get back 45 minutes every Friday and stop thinking about it, that's worth it. I've scaled a company before — I know what 45 minutes of founder time is worth.

**Me:** What would make you not use it?

**Them:** If it required me to do a lot of setup, or if it sent me a finished update without me asking. I want a document I fill in, not a robot that pretends to be me.

---

## Notes from this interview

**Second-time founders have higher WTP and sharper opinions:** He's willing to pay $150-200/month and can clearly articulate what he wants. He's been burned by tools before (tried some, none worked) so he knows what failure looks like.

**Friday not Sunday:** Update day shifts as founders get more experienced. The tool needs to work whenever the founder does it, not assume Sunday.

**The "pre-loaded document" framing:** This is the best product description I've heard from an interviewee. Data pre-loaded, founder writes. Not content generation, not AI writing. Just data in the right place. This language is worth using in product copy.

**Trust threshold is lower for experienced founders:** He can spot a wrong number, so he's less worried about being deceived by automation. "I can catch that before I send" is very different from the skepticism in Interview #6.

**Anti-feature: sending without asking.** If the tool ever auto-sends or auto-drafts without explicit invitation, he'd walk. The writing is his process for thinking, not output to be automated.

**WTP consistent with earlier Series A/experienced founders:** $150-200/month. Stage and experience both seem to predict WTP more than company size.
