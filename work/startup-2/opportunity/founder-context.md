## The startup
Modern agriculture runs on chemicals — and that model is hitting diminishing returns.
Pesticide residues are everywhere (food, water, soil) and people worldwide worry about
long-term health risks like glyphosate. Meanwhile pests and weeds keep evolving, so
farmers spray more, costs rise, margins fall, and the new-chemical pipeline is slower and
more expensive than ever. Farmers are trapped: use more chemicals → diminishing results →
pay more → take on more risk — but they can't stop, because if pests win, crops die.

Several things changed at once that make this newly solvable:
- **AI can now see** — identify individual weeds and pests in real time.
- **Sensors and cameras got cheap** enough to put everywhere.
- **Robotics can act with precision** — treat one plant instead of blanketing a field.
- **Biology is catching up** — microbes, peptides, RNA-based solutions, and engineered
  plants can replace whole classes of synthetic chemicals. Real science breakthroughs
  will be augmented by AGI.

The thesis: agriculture is one of the biggest markets in the world. A company that cuts
pesticide use by ~90% while helping farmers grow more food lowers cost and raises yield
at the same time — so adoption isn't slow, it's explosive. That's a generational company.

Building tools that help farmers produce more with fewer chemicals.
