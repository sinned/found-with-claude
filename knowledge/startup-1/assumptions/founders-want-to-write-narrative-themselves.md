# Founders want to write the investor update narrative themselves — not have AI write it for them

The product should automate data gathering only, not narrative generation. Both early
interviewees said the writing is the part they value. Building an AI that writes the
update solves the wrong problem and may not be trusted.

**Validation status:** unvalidated — directionally supported by 2 interviews but not
explicitly probed as a question. It is possible that founders say they want to write
but would accept AI writing if the quality were high enough.

**What "validated" looks like:** 4+ of 5 next interviewees say "I want to write it
myself even if the gathering is automated" when directly asked.

**What "invalidated" looks like:** Multiple interviewees say "I'd actually be fine
with AI writing a draft if it's good" or "I only want to write if I don't have a
better option." This would be a significant product pivot signal — maybe the
right product IS a full AI-written update.

**How we'll test it:** In the next 5 interviews, ask directly: "If the gathering was
automated, would you want the writing automated too, or would you still want to write
it yourself?" Listen for hedging — "I guess I'd still want to" is different from "I
definitely want to."

**Current evidence:** Both interviewees mentioned enjoying the writing or finding it
valuable. Quote: "The writing I actually like. It forces me to think." But neither
was asked directly whether they would accept AI-written updates.
