# Founders spend 3x more time gathering data than writing the update itself

In both initial interviews, the person described spending 90-120 minutes gathering
metrics from 4-6 sources before writing a 30-minute investor update. The writing
is the valuable, thoughtful part. The gathering is mechanical, dreaded, and the
dominant time cost.

**Evidence:** 2/2 initial interviews unprompted described this 80/20 split. Best
quote: "Right now I'm spending 80% of my time on data plumbing and 20% on 'what
does this mean.'" The founder confirmed the same pattern from personal experience
at two companies.

**Implication for product:** Automate the gathering, not the writing. The product
should produce ready-to-use numbers with sources, not a draft narrative. Founders
want to write — the gathering is the pain.

**Open question:** Is the 80/20 split consistent across company stages? Interview #1
was seed-stage. Does a Series A founder with a dedicated ops person have the same
split?
