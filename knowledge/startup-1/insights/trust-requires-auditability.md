# Source attribution is the minimum viable trust mechanism — not a nice-to-have

Founders will not send AI-gathered numbers to investors without being able to trace
every number back to its source. This is not a product preference — it is tied to
professional identity. Sending wrong numbers to investors is a professional risk with
career implications, not just an accuracy issue.

**Evidence:** 2/2 interviews described trust as the core barrier. Interview #2 used
the word "auditable" unprompted before it was introduced. Key quote: "I should be
able to click on the MRR number and see where it came from." Another quote: "If I'm
presenting wrong numbers, what's my job?" The fear is not an error — it is what an
error means about them.

**Implication for product:** Inline source attribution (click a number, see the
source) is not a v2 feature. It is the core trust mechanism that makes the product
usable at all. Build this in v0.

**Open question:** What is the trust threshold? Is a source link enough, or do
founders also need to see the calculation? Probe in the next 5 interviews: "At what
point would you send automated numbers to investors without double-checking?"
