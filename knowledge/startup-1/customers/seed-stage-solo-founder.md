# Seed-stage solo (or near-solo) founder sending weekly investor updates

Founder of a B2B SaaS company at seed stage, typically 1-5 people. Sends investor
updates weekly or bi-weekly. Does not have a dedicated ops person or finance hire.
Does not have a BI tool that covers all their sources. Personally responsible for
the accuracy of every number in the update.

**Job to be done:** When I sit down to write my investor update, I want the right
numbers already gathered and sourced, so I can spend my time on the writing and
thinking — not the mechanical data collection.

**How they describe the pain:** "The writing I actually like. It forces me to think.
The gathering is just tedious." (Interview #1)

**What they currently use:** Google Docs or Notion as the update template. Manual
pulls from Stripe, Mercury, bank account, Mixpanel or Amplitude, Linear or GitHub,
sometimes Salesforce or HubSpot. No integration layer that covers all sources.

**Why they'd switch:** If they trusted the gathered numbers. The threshold for trust
is the ability to trace every number to its source inline — click through to see the
raw data. Speed of setup matters: founders will not spend 8 hours integrating a tool
that saves them 2 hours per update.

**Evidence:** 2 initial interviews, plus founder's own experience. Sample is too
small for confident conclusions — treat as primary hypothesis to validate with 5+
more interviews.
