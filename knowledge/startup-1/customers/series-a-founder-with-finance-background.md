# Series A founder or finance leader with strong data accountability standards

Founder or finance-adjacent operator at Series A+ company with a background that
makes data accuracy a professional identity issue, not just a preference. May have
dedicated ops or finance staff, but still personally checks numbers before any
investor or leadership communication.

**Job to be done:** When I prepare a metric for investor or leadership review, I want
to know exactly where it came from and that it is current, so I can present it without
second-guessing myself.

**How they describe the pain:** "If I'm presenting wrong numbers, what's my job?"
The fear is tied to professional identity — a wrong number is not just an error, it
is a signal of incompetence.

**What they currently use:** May have BI tools (Looker, Metabase) for operational
reporting but still do investor/leadership reporting manually because BI tools don't
provide the auditability they need for high-stakes communication.

**Why they'd switch:** Inline source attribution is the bar. This customer needs to
be able to click on any number and see the raw source data. They are potentially
willing to pay more than the seed-stage founder because the professional risk is higher.

**Evidence:** Interview #2 (the word "auditable" came up unprompted). Sample is very
small — 1 interview. This may be a secondary persona or a variation on the primary.
Needs more research before treating as a distinct segment.
