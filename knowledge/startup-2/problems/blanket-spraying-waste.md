# Farmers who spray fields uniformly waste most of their chemical on bare soil and healthy plants

Conventional application blankets an entire field to kill scattered weeds or pests, so the
majority of chemical lands where it isn't needed. This wastes money, increases residues in
food/water/soil, and contributes to resistance pressure. Treating individual plants
(see-and-spray, laser/mechanical weeding) can cut chemical use dramatically.

**Who has it:** Row-crop farmers with large acreage and significant per-acre chemical spend.
**Severity:** Chemical is a major variable cost; precision approaches claim large reductions.
Also drives the residue problem regulators and buyers care about.
**Current workarounds:** Variable-rate spraying (coarse), or premium precision hardware
(John Deere See & Spray, Carbon Robotics) that is expensive and crop-limited.
**Evidence:** Founder thesis + known precision-ag market; not yet validated with farmers.
