# Farmers who rely on chemical pest/weed control suffer from a resistance treadmill: what worked stops working, so they spray more for diminishing results

Pests and weeds evolve resistance, so the same chemicals lose efficacy season over season.
Farmers respond by spraying more and switching products, which raises input costs and
accelerates resistance further. The new-chemical R&D pipeline is too slow and expensive to
stay ahead, so the loop tightens over time.

**Who has it:** Row-crop farmers and the agronomists who advise them; anyone whose margins
depend on chemical-based pest/weed control.
**Severity:** Rising input costs and falling margins each season; existential crop-loss risk
if pests win. Compounds over time — each season of over-spraying worsens the next.
**Current workarounds:** Spray more, rotate products, lean on agronomist/retailer advice
(the retailer is conflicted — they sell the chemical).
**Evidence:** Founder thesis only — not yet validated with farmers. Needs discovery.
