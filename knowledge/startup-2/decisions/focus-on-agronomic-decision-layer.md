# Pursue the agronomic decision layer (resistance management + spray optimization) as a learning beachhead — not yet a committed company

**Made:** 2026-06-14
**Status:** active

Of five opportunity areas in reduced-chemical agriculture, the decision-layer software play
ranked highest (11/15) — not because it has the biggest prize (precision robotics and
biologicals do) but because it is the most learnable and capital-light path for an
idea-stage founder with no agriculture domain expertise. The gating risk across all areas
is weak founder-market fit; the fastest way to upgrade it is cheap, fast customer contact,
which a software-only decision tool enables. Treat this as a beachhead for building an edge,
not a final commitment.

**Alternatives considered:**
- Precision detection-and-treatment hardware (10/15) — highest pain, but capital-intensive,
  contested by well-funded incumbents, poor fit for a solo idea-stage founder.
- Biological crop protection (9/15) — needs deep bio + wet lab + regulatory; needs a science
  co-founder.
- Engineered crops (8/15) — 5–10 yr cycles, regulatory and distribution moats; "later" bet.
- Residue/compliance transparency (8/15) — customer is the retailer/regulator, not the
  farmer; different company than the rest of the thesis.

**What would cause us to revisit this:** Discovery shows farmers/agronomists won't change
spraying behavior based on software (adoption fails); OR a software-only wedge into precision
detection (#2) proves more attractive; OR a science co-founder joins, opening the bio/genetics
paths.
