# No system holds field-by-field resistance history or flags mode-of-action stacking; the "resistance brain" lives in human memory and duct-taped spreadsheets

Existing ag software (FieldView, co-op platforms) maps fields and processes orders but does
not reason about resistance. Agronomists track which fields have resistant weeds, and which
modes of action have been overused, in spreadsheets and their own heads — and that knowledge
walks out the door when senior agronomists retire.

**Evidence:** 4/5 simulated interviews. Tyler: "FieldView... does not tell me 'you've run
Group 14 three passes in a row, you're cooking yourself.'" Maria tracks resistance in a
color-coded spreadsheet; Greg: "that knowledge isn't in any software. It's in me." Greg also
flags retirement as a looming institutional-knowledge loss for co-ops.
**Implication for product:** The differentiated wedge is a resistance-aware decision/memory
layer on top of existing precision and farm-management tools — explicitly NOT another mapping
app. Co-op knowledge-capture (continuity across retiring agronomists) may be the strongest
enterprise value.
**Open question:** Is the wedge resistance decisioning, logistics/execution, or knowledge
capture? The panel split three ways.
