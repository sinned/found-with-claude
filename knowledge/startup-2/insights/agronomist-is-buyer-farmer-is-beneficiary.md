# The farmer is the beneficiary of reduced-chemical tools, but the agronomist is the user and the co-op/consultant is the buyer

Across the panel, farmers do not make spray decisions from software — they trust an
agronomist (independent or co-op) who holds the plan, and they execute on relationship.
Farmers actively reject "another app." This separates three roles that are easy to conflate:
beneficiary (farmer), user (agronomist), buyer/check-signer (co-op or independent consultant).

**Evidence:** 5/5 simulated interviews. Maria (independent CCA): "The grower buys *me*. I'm
the customer." Greg (co-op): "Figure out who signs the check. It's not the farmer." Dale
(farmer): "I'm still gonna call Kevin."
**Implication for product:** Build for the agronomist as the user and sell to the co-op or
consultant as the buyer; treat the farmer as beneficiary, not customer. A direct-to-farmer
product likely fails.
**Open question:** Of the two buyers (conflicted co-op with budget vs aligned independent with
little budget), which actually converts? — This is the riskiest open assumption.

> ⚠️ Derived from SIMULATED interviews. Hypothesis, not validated evidence.
