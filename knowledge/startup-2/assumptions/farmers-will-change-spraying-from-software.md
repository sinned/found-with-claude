# Farmers (or their agronomists) will change real spraying decisions based on software recommendations

**Validation status:** unvalidated
We believe a resistance-aware decision tool can cut chemical use — but only if the user
actually acts on it. Spraying decisions today are driven by habit, risk-aversion, and
trusted retailer/agronomist relationships (the retailer is conflicted because it sells the
chemical). Software has to overcome all of that.

**What "validated" looks like:** Farmers/agronomists in discovery describe willingness to
change product, rate, or timing based on a tool — and ideally point to a tool they already
trust for similar decisions.
**What "invalidated" looks like:** They say the decision is relationship-driven or
habit-driven and no software would move it; or they nod politely but can't name a behavior
they'd change.
**How we'll test it:** 8–10 customer discovery interviews with row-crop farmers and
independent agronomists about how spraying decisions actually get made today.
**Current evidence:** None — founder thesis only.
