# An explainable resistance recommendation is trustworthy enough that a consultant will sign their name to it

**Validation status:** unvalidated
The consultant's reputation and liability ride on every recommendation. They will not put
their name on a black box. The product thesis is that a transparent, editable, reasoned
recommendation clears that bar — making explainability the core of the product, not a feature.

**What "validated" looks like:** A real CCA reviews an AgroGuard recommendation on their own
fields and says they'd sign it / send it to a grower as-is or lightly edited.
**What "invalidated" looks like:** They can't follow the reasoning, don't trust it, or always
override it so heavily the tool adds no value.
**How we'll test it:** Put a prototype recommendation in front of a real consultant's real
field data and watch whether they'd act on it.
**Current evidence:** Panel archetypes (independent CCA, new agronomist) demanded
explainability as a gating requirement — from simulated data only.
