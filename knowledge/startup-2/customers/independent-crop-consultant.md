# Independent crop consultant (fee-for-service CCA)

A certified crop adviser who sells advice, not product, for a per-acre retainer (~$8–14/ac).
Advises dozens of growers across tens of thousands of acres. Independence is their value prop:
they're the person in the room who makes no money on what's in the tank. Capacity-capped by
how much resistance/field history they can hold in their head.

**Job to be done:** When I'm managing dozens of growers across thousands of acres, I want to
hold each field's resistance history and catch mode-of-action stacking, so I can serve more
acres without my quality or reputation slipping.
**How they describe the pain:** "I'm stitching it together in my head... I physically can't
take more growers because I can't hold more in my head."
**What they currently use:** A scouting app, per-grower spreadsheets, bookmarked chemical
labels, a personal color-coded resistance spreadsheet.
**Why they'd switch:** A tool that lifts their acre ceiling or lets them charge more for
better service — but only if recommendations are explainable (their liability/reputation) and
survive real-world mess (substitutions, weather, non-compliance).
**Evidence:** Simulated interview 02 (Maria Olvera). Most aligned incentive in the panel, but
small per-seat budget; market is a few thousand, fragmented.
