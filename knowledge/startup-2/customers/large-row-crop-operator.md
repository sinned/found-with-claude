# Large row-crop operator (multi-thousand-acre, team-run)

A grower running thousands of acres with a management team, spray crews, and an independent
consultant already retained. The agronomy decision is largely solved; the unmet pain is
execution under shrinking weather and regulatory (e.g., dicamba label) windows, plus clean
records for liability.

**Job to be done:** When I have a narrow weather/regulatory window and thousands of acres, I
want the application sequence that protects the most yield and stays inside the label, so I
don't lose a good plan to logistics — and clean records if a drift complaint comes.
**How they describe the pain:** "I might have the perfect resistance-rotation plan on paper,
but if I can't cover the acres before the pigweed's too big or the rain comes... the plan's
worthless."
**What they currently use:** Independent consultant for agronomy; own sprayers/crews for
execution; spray logs that "don't always match" the consultant's records.
**Why they'd switch:** Logistics optimization across the footprint and audit-clean as-applied
records for liability protection — value here is operational, not agronomic.
**Evidence:** Simulated interview 04 (Coleman Reyes). Warns explicitly: do NOT build one
product for both small and large farms — "same disease, different symptoms."
