# Farmer-owned co-op agronomy division (the trusted-but-conflicted channel)

A regional cooperative that sells seed/chemical/fertilizer/application and employs the
agronomists farmers trust most. Member-owned, so repeat-trust and local accountability partly
offset the conflict of selling what it recommends. Holds the trusted distribution into every
farm county — and faces senior-agronomist retirements that threaten institutional knowledge.

**Job to be done:** When my senior agronomists retire, I want to capture their territory
knowledge and keep program quality consistent across agronomists, so I can defend against
independents and keep my members.
**How they describe the pain:** "Thirty years of 'this field, this weed, this history' walks
out the door... that knowledge isn't in any software. It's in me."
**What they currently use:** A co-op agronomy/sales platform good for transactions and field
records but not resistance reasoning; the rest is in agronomists' heads.
**Why they'd switch:** Continuity, consistency, and defensibility vs independents — IF the
tool feeds the order rather than replacing it and is positioned as "make your chemistries last
longer," NOT "sell less chemical."
**Evidence:** Simulated interview 05 (Greg Lindqvist). Has the budget (enterprise buyer) but a
revenue model the product partially undermines — the core go-to-market tension.
