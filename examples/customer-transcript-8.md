# Customer Interview Transcript — Interview #8

*Interviewee: Founder & CEO, seed-stage company (~6 employees)*
*Date: 2026-06-14*
*Format: 25-minute call*
*Context: Does NOT send regular investor updates — sends them ad hoc*

---

**Me:** How often do you send investor updates?

**Them:** When I have something to say. So sometimes twice in a month, sometimes not for six weeks. I've never been on a fixed cadence.

**Me:** Are your investors okay with that?

**Them:** Most of them. I have two angels who are pretty hands-off. I have one seed fund that has asked me to do it monthly, and I've been bad about it. I keep meaning to and then I don't.

**Me:** What gets in the way?

**Them:** Honestly? I don't think the updates add much value. When something real happens I email them directly. When things are just... progressing... I feel like a formal update is kind of performative.

**Me:** [This is a different mental model. He doesn't see the value of regular updates, which is upstream of the data gathering problem.]

**Me:** The seed fund that asked for monthly — what's their reasoning?

**Them:** They said they want to be useful to me. Which I appreciate in theory. But the way they're useful to me is over the phone, not through an update. The update feels like I'm writing a report for my boss, which I don't love.

**Me:** When you have sent formal updates, what goes in them?

**Them:** Stripe MRR, cash position, what we're working on, what I need. Pretty standard.

**Me:** How long does that take to put together?

**Them:** Maybe 30 minutes? I don't have a lot of metrics to gather. We're early.

**Me:** Is the data gathering hard for you?

**Them:** Not really. I know where everything lives. It's more that I have to context-switch into "update mode" and that feels like a chore.

**Me:** [The pain here is different — not the gathering, but the mindset shift required. Or maybe he just doesn't have enough data sources yet to feel the gathering pain.]

**Me:** If there was a tool that gathered your data automatically and had the template pre-filled, would that change your update frequency?

**Them:** [pause] Maybe? If it was already done when I woke up, I might send it more. The friction of starting is probably part of why I don't.

**Me:** What do you mean by "already done"?

**Them:** Like, I open an email or a document and the numbers are already there and I just have to read them and add context. If I had to go set up a tool, it wouldn't help — I'd never set it up.

**Me:** Would you pay for something like that?

**Them:** Probably not right now. We're really early. If I had a board and felt real pressure to send more frequently, maybe. Right now it's not a pain I feel.

---

## Notes from this interview

**The non-customer:** This founder doesn't send regular updates and doesn't feel the pain acutely. This is important — not everyone in the "founder" category is in the target market. The pain correlates with: (a) active investors who expect updates, (b) multiple data sources, (c) a fixed update cadence.

**The frequency problem is upstream of the data problem:** He doesn't send updates because he doesn't see the value — not because the data gathering is hard. Solving data gathering won't change his behavior.

**Low data volume = low pain:** Only a few data sources, only 6 employees. The gathering isn't that hard yet. This suggests the product finds PMF at a certain scale threshold.

**The "already done" framing:** Even this non-customer described his ideal as "I open it and it's already done." The framing of reducing the starting-is-hard friction resonates, even if he wouldn't pay for it now.

**ICP sharpening:** Target founders who: (1) have committed to a regular cadence and feel the obligation, (2) have 3+ data sources to pull from, (3) have investors who expect consistent updates. This founder has none of those three.
