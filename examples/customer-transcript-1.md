# Customer Interview Transcript — Interview #1

*Interviewee: Founder, Series A SaaS company (~40 employees)*
*Date: 2026-06-10*
*Format: 35-minute video call*
*Interviewer notes in [brackets]*

---

**Me:** Thanks for making time. I'm not pitching anything — I'm doing research on a 
problem I think might be worth solving. The most helpful thing you can do is be 
really honest, especially when my assumptions are wrong.

**Them:** Sure, happy to help.

**Me:** Walk me through what your Sunday evenings typically look like.

**Them:** [laughs] Oh god. Yeah so — investor update day. It's always Sunday for 
some reason. I sit down at like 7pm and I need to send the update by 9pm. And the 
first hour and a half is just pulling data.

**Me:** What do you mean, pulling data?

**Them:** Like — I have to go to Stripe and get the MRR number. Then I go to 
Mixpanel to get the weekly active users. Then I look at my bank account in Mercury 
to figure out the cash position and the burn rate. And then I have our Linear 
board to see what the team shipped this week. And I have to just... manually look 
at all of these things and type them into a Google Doc.

**Me:** How long does that take?

**Them:** The data gathering? Probably 90 minutes. The actual writing takes like 30 minutes.

**Me:** [This is interesting — the write is fast, the gather is slow] Have you tried 
to automate any of this?

**Them:** I tried using Zapier to auto-pull the Stripe number into a spreadsheet, 
and that kind of worked, but then I had to remember to look at the spreadsheet 
instead of going directly to Stripe, and... I don't know, it broke twice and I 
just went back to doing it manually.

**Me:** What did your investors actually want to see in the update?

**Them:** Basically: MRR and growth, cash and burn, top 3 wins, top 3 problems, 
what I'm asking them for. That's it. It's always the same format. I've been using 
the same format for 18 months.

**Me:** How often do you change the format?

**Them:** Never. My investors actually get annoyed if I change it. They have a 
cadence of what they expect.

**Me:** [Interesting — the format is stable, which means the data gathering is 
highly predictable. Same sources, same format, every week.]

**Me:** If this was solved perfectly — the data gathering just... happened — what 
would you do with the extra 90 minutes on Sunday?

**Them:** [long pause] Honestly? I would use it to actually think about the business. 
The writing part is valuable — I have to articulate where we are and what I'm worried 
about. The gathering part is just... drudgery.

**Me:** Would you pay for something that automated this?

**Them:** What would it cost?

**Me:** Just hypothetically — what would it be worth?

**Them:** If it actually worked and I trusted the numbers? Honestly, yes. I think 
so. I mean, I pay $200/month for things that save me way less time. This is like 
6 hours a month I would get back.

**Me:** [Did not push on specific price, but got a positive signal]

**Me:** Do you know other founders who have this problem?

**Them:** Oh, definitely. We have a founder Slack group — we commiserate about 
investor updates all the time. It's like a universal founder experience. I don't 
think anyone enjoys it.

**Me:** Last question — is there anything you expected me to ask that I didn't?

**Them:** I guess I expected you to ask about the writing. But you're right that 
it's the gathering. The writing I actually like. It forces me to think. The 
gathering just... there's nothing to it. It's just tedious.

---

## Notes from this interview

**Most important thing heard:** "The writing I actually like. It forces me to think. 
The gathering is just tedious." → This is important. The product should NOT automate 
the writing — just the gathering. Founders want to write. They do not want to gather.

**Surprising thing:** The format never changes. Same format for 18 months. Same 
sources. This means the automation is highly predictable — no AI needed for the 
gathering, just reliable integration with 4 sources.

**Direct quote worth preserving:** "If it actually worked and I trusted the numbers" — 
the trust component is key. The fear is not that the gathering is hard — it's that 
the gathered numbers will be wrong and they will send wrong data to investors.

**Referrals:** "We have a founder Slack group" — asked for access, they said they 
would think about it. Follow up.

**Next question to explore:** What would it take for them to trust AI-gathered numbers 
enough to send them to investors without double-checking? This is the hardest part.
