# Customer Interview Transcript — Interview #5

*Interviewee: Founder & CEO, Series A company (~55 employees)*
*Date: 2026-06-13*
*Format: 35-minute call*
*Context: Has a Chief of Staff who handles parts of the update process*

---

**Me:** I understand you have some operational support for investor updates — how does that process work for you?

**Them:** Yeah, my Chief of Staff drafts the update. She has a checklist of what data to pull and where to find it, and she puts together the first draft, and then I review and edit and send.

**Me:** Walk me through her checklist.

**Them:** Stripe for MRR and new ARR. Mercury for cash and burn. HubSpot for pipeline — we track deals in HubSpot now. And then I give her a list of the 3-5 things I want to highlight, and she weaves it together.

**Me:** How long does it take her?

**Them:** She says 2-3 hours but I think it's more like 4. And she only does it once a month — we do monthly updates, not weekly.

**Me:** What happens in those 2-4 hours?

**Them:** The data gathering is probably half of it. Honestly the thing I didn't expect is how often she comes back to me with questions. "Is this the right MRR number or should I use this other one?" "There's a discrepancy between HubSpot and the contract we signed — which do I use?" She's new to SaaS metrics so there's a lot of back and forth.

**Me:** [The coordination cost is real — not just the time to gather, but the overhead of handing that task to someone who doesn't have full context.]

**Me:** How do you handle those discrepancies yourself when she escalates them?

**Them:** I usually just make a judgment call. Like, "use the Stripe number, not the HubSpot number, because HubSpot gets updated late." But now she has to know that rule, and the next question is slightly different, and... we probably have 20 of these undocumented rules.

**Me:** Have you written them down?

**Them:** [laughs] No. She has taken notes but it's not really a system.

**Me:** What would a better version of this process look like?

**Them:** Ideally, she opens something, the data is already there and clearly sourced, and she just has to write. The back-and-forth questions about data would go away.

**Me:** Would you be comfortable with a tool that auto-pulled this data if every number had a source link attached?

**Them:** That would actually solve the problem for me. Because right now when she asks me "is this the right MRR number," I have to go look myself. If the number says "from Stripe, pulled at 11pm Sunday, here are the subscription IDs," she can evaluate that herself.

**Me:** What would you pay for a tool that did this?

**Them:** If it saves 2 hours of my Chief of Staff's time per month... I'd pay at least $200/month. Maybe more if it also handled the HubSpot data, but I know that's harder.

**Me:** What would make you not buy it?

**Them:** If it required a lot of setup or maintenance. We are already stretched on ops bandwidth. If it just worked and connected to Stripe and Mercury on day one, that's the bar.

**Me:** Are your investors happy with monthly updates?

**Them:** Mostly. We have one very active board member who keeps asking for more frequent data. I keep pushing back because once a month is already a lot of work.

---

## Notes from this interview

**The delegation problem:** At Series A, the update task often gets delegated. But delegation creates a new problem: coordination overhead. The delegate doesn't have the mental model needed to resolve data ambiguities, so they escalate constantly.

**Undocumented rules:** "Use Stripe not HubSpot for MRR" is a judgment that's been made but never written down. If the tool could encode these rules (or at least make the source transparent enough that the delegate can figure it out), it would unlock a lot of value.

**Different buyer than Interview #1:** The CEO of Interview #1 was the one doing the work. Here, the CEO feels the overhead through the Chief of Staff escalations. The pain is one step removed.

**Higher WTP:** $200+/month at Series A. Consistent with Interview #1 (also Series A range) and higher than Interview #3 (seed).

**The "just works" bar:** Setup and maintenance overhead is a deal-breaker for this segment. HubSpot is a gap but an understandable one — Stripe + Mercury first.

**Active board member dynamic:** Pressure to increase update frequency is coming from investors, not founders. Founders would send less often if they could.
