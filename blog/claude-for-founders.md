# Claude for Founders: More Than a Coding Assistant

*A positioning piece for this skill pack*

---

Every demo of AI-for-founders shows the same thing: a founder types a prompt,
Claude writes a React component, the product appears on screen.

This is impressive. It is also a fraction of the founder's job.

---

## What founders actually do

In the first year of a startup, a founder's time breaks down roughly like this:

- **40%** — Talking to customers, synthesizing what they hear, deciding what to build
- **25%** — Building (or managing people who build)
- **20%** — Selling, recruiting, fundraising
- **15%** — Everything else

The AI coding demos address the 25%. This skill pack addresses the 40%.

And the 40% is where most founding decisions get made — and where most founding
mistakes happen.

---

## The questions that matter

Before a founder writes a single line of code, they face questions like:

**Is this problem real?** Not "could this be a business" — does this specific pain
exist, does it hurt enough, are people already spending money or time to solve it?

**Who has it?** Not a demographic segment — a specific person, in a specific situation,
with a specific current workflow that is failing them.

**What are they already doing?** The workaround is the market. What do people do
today tells you more about the problem than anything else.

**What should we build first?** Not "what is the vision" — what is the single thing
we could build in a week that would tell us if we are right?

**What is the riskiest assumption?** Every startup thesis has a bet that, if wrong,
makes the whole thing collapse. Finding that bet before spending 6 months building
is the most important thing a founder can do.

---

## Where Claude comes in

None of these questions require code. They require:
- Synthesizing messy information fast
- Finding patterns across multiple data sources
- Producing structured artifacts from unstructured input
- Pressure-testing ideas before committing to them
- Maintaining clarity under uncertainty

These are exactly what Claude is good at.

A founder who has just done 7 customer interviews has 7 documents of varying quality,
full of tangents and irrelevant conversation. Running `/customer-discovery-synthesizer`
on those 7 documents produces a synthesis in minutes: the patterns that appeared in
3+ interviews, the most important quotes, the jobs-to-be-done, and the one thing
that is still unknown.

A founder who has a product concept but is not sure it will land can run
`/synthetic-customer-panel` to see how different customer archetypes react — not
as a replacement for real interviews, but as preparation. The panel will surface
the objections that the founder has not thought to prepare for.

A founder at Step 10 who has to fundraise can run `/fundraising-narrative` against
their actual traction data and get an investor memo with an objection map that
shows exactly where the story is weakest.

## The compress-the-loop thesis

Startups win by learning faster than everyone else. The company that goes from
"I have a hunch" to "I know this is real and I know what to build" in 2 weeks
beats the one that takes 6 months.

Every skill in this pack is designed to compress one phase of the founder's learning
loop:
- Turn messy notes into a ranked opportunity map in 30 minutes instead of 3 days
- Turn 7 interview transcripts into a structured synthesis in 10 minutes instead of a week
- Turn a product concept into a stress-tested brief in an afternoon instead of a month

The bottleneck becomes learning and judgment — not writing and synthesis.

---

## What this is not

This is not magic. The skills cannot:
- Replace talking to customers (they can only synthesize what was heard)
- Validate demand (only real behavior validates demand)
- Make a weak thesis strong (they can only surface that it is weak faster)
- Write code (prototype-with-claude handles that)

These are thinking tools for founders who have already done the hard work of talking
to real people, observing real behavior, and making real decisions under real
uncertainty. The tools compress the synthesis. The judgment still belongs to the founder.

## The pattern that generalizes

`founding-with-claude` is one instance of a pattern: a domain-specific Claude
Skills pack that encodes expertise in a professional workflow.

`prototype-with-claude` encodes the AI feature prototyping workflow.
`founding-with-claude` encodes the company-building workflow.

The pattern works because Claude Code skills are just plain-text job descriptions.
Any professional workflow that can be described clearly can be a skill. Any domain
where expertise can be encoded in structured steps and output formats is a candidate
for a skills pack.

This is not about making Claude a general assistant. It is about making Claude
a specialist — one that knows the specific steps, the specific questions, and the
specific outputs that a founder needs at each phase of building a company.

That specificity is what makes it useful.
