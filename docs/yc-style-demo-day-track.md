# YC-Style Demo Day Track

How to use this skill pack in the context of a YC-style accelerator program —
or any intensive 10-12 week company-building sprint.

---

## The YC model and this skill pack

YC runs 3-month batches. The arc:
- Weeks 1-2: figure out what to build (or build faster if you already know)
- Weeks 3-8: build and get users
- Weeks 9-10: figure out what your metrics mean and get more users
- Weeks 11-12: Demo Day prep

This skill pack maps onto that arc. But it adds structure to phases that YC
leaves relatively unstructured — especially the problem discovery and PMF
analysis phases.

## The Demo Day preparation track

If you are in week 9 and Demo Day is in 2 weeks:

**Week 9:**
1. Run `/pmf-signal-review` — you need to know your signals cold before writing the narrative
2. Run `/fundraising-narrative` — build the investor memo first, Demo Day script second
3. Share the memo with 2-3 trusted people for feedback
4. Revise

**Week 10:**
1. Run `/demo-day-script` from the revised narrative
2. Write 5 hook variations, test them with someone not in your world
3. Practice the script 10+ times out loud (not in your head — out loud)
4. Record yourself and watch it once
5. Get timing feedback from a coach or advisor

**Demo Day:**
- The script is the floor, not the ceiling — know it cold so you can deliver above it
- The Q&A after is often more important than the presentation — prepare for the
  3 hardest questions

## What YC partners want to see on Demo Day

Based on public YC guidance and alumni accounts:

**In the 2-minute presentation:**
1. What you do (in one sentence, not jargon)
2. The problem (specific, painful, relatable)
3. Your solution (what it does, not how it works)
4. Traction (the most impressive number you have)
5. The ask (how much, to do what)

**In the Q&A:**
- Why you (founder-market fit)
- Why now (what changed)
- Biggest risk (honest answer preferred over deflection)
- What happens if [big competitor] does this

## The common Demo Day failure modes

**Spending too long on the problem.** If you are not at the solution by 45 seconds,
you have lost them. Trust that the problem slide (if you have slides) or your first
sentence communicates the pain.

**Explaining how the technology works.** Investors do not need to understand how
your AI works. They need to believe it works. Use the output as evidence, not
the architecture.

**Weak traction numbers.** "We have 50 users and growing" is weak. "We have 50 users,
70% are still active after 4 weeks, and 3 referred someone without being asked" is
strong. The difference is specificity and signal.

**A generic ask.** "We're raising $1M to grow the team" is generic. "We're raising
$1M to reach 500 active users and launch our Stripe integration, which our 8 biggest
users have been asking for since Day 1" is specific and credible.

## How to prepare for the partners meeting (group office hours)

Before every group office hours or partner meeting, run `/weekly-founder-review`.
The output gives you:
- A clear summary of what happened this week
- The metric that moved (or did not move) and why
- The one question you are most uncertain about
- The one thing you are doing next week

Partner meetings go better when founders come in knowing what happened, what it
means, and what they are going to do about it — not just reporting that they are
"making progress."

## The difference between this track and just using Claude Code for coding

Founders in YC who use Claude Code primarily for writing code will be faster
at building. Founders who use this skill pack will be faster at:
- Finding the right problem before building
- Synthesizing what customers told them
- Deciding what to build next
- Reading whether the PMF signals are real
- Preparing the narrative for Demo Day

The prototype-with-claude repo handles the coding acceleration. This repo handles
the company-building acceleration. They are designed to be used together.
