## Interview structure (suggested — 30-45 minutes)

### Opening (5 min)
Set context: "I am not pitching anything. I am trying to understand the problem
before building. The most helpful thing you can do is be honest, especially when
things I am thinking are wrong."

### The job (10 min)
- Walk me through your typical [week / day / process around the problem area].
- Where does that process go wrong or slow down?
- Show me (if applicable) — can you pull up what you actually use right now?

### The problem (10 min)
- When does this bother you most?
- How much time does it actually take?
- What have you tried to fix it?
- What happened when you tried that?

### The workaround (5 min)
- What are you doing today that you wish you did not have to do?
- How much would you pay to not do it? (do not ask directly — observe reaction)
- Who else has this problem? (warm referral question)

### The dream (5 min)
- If this was solved perfectly, what would that look like?
- What would you stop doing if this was solved?
- Who else cares about this at your company?

### Close (5 min)
- Is there anything you expected me to ask that I did not?
- Is there someone else I should talk to?

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Pitching before listening (they will tell you what you want to hear)
- Asking hypothetical questions ("would you use this?") — ask behavioral ones
- Moving on too quickly when they describe a problem — go deeper
- Forgetting to ask "what do you do today?" — the workaround is the market
- Ending without asking for a referral — warm intros are gold at this stage
