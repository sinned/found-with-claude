# Weekly Founder Review

The weekly founder review is the one skill that runs every week, regardless of
what phase you are in. It asks three questions:

1. What did we learn this week?
2. What did we ship or do?
3. What is the single most important thing to do next week?

This is the glue skill. It connects every other skill in the pack.

## Input

The founder should bring whatever happened this week:
- Wins and things that worked
- Blocks, failures, or things that did not work
- Customer conversations or signals
- Product or prototype updates
- Personal state (burned out? energized? uncertain?)

Drop it all in the current conversation or in `examples/messy-founder-notes.md`.
The more raw the input, the better the synthesis.

Also look for:
- Recent outputs from other skills (opportunity memo, synthesis, PMF review)
- The previous week's review output if it exists
- Any goals or bets that were set last week

## Steps

1. **Capture the week** — Read all input. Do not filter yet. Every signal is data.

2. **Extract what was learned** — Separate observations (what happened) from
   conclusions (what it means). Both matter. Most founders conflate them.

3. **Extract what was done** — What was shipped, tested, sent, or decided?
   Be specific. "Worked on the product" is not a deliverable.

4. **Extract what was heard from users/customers** — Direct quotes, behavioral
   signals, unexpected things people said or did. This section often has the
   most important signal.

5. **Identify the surprise** — What happened this week that the founder did not
   expect? Surprises are usually the most important data points.

6. **Assess the riskiest assumption** — Based on this week's learning, what is
   the most important thing the founder still does not know? This should be the
   single most important question to answer next week.

7. **Decide the one thing** — What is the single most important thing to do next
   week? Not a list. One thing. If the founder cannot name it, help them decide.

8. **Calibrate the founder's state** — Is the founder energized, depleted, uncertain,
   or clear? Name it. Founders who are depleted make worse decisions. If the state
   is poor, that is worth surfacing.

9. **Write the review** — Follow the output format below.

10. **Save output** — Write to `examples/weekly-review-[date].md`.

## Output format

```markdown
# Weekly Founder Review
*Week of [date] | Phase: [current phase in the journey]*

## What we learned

**Observations (what happened):**
- [Specific thing observed — user did X, metric moved Y, conversation revealed Z]
- [Another observation...]

**Conclusions (what it means):**
- [Conclusion 1 — be specific and honest about confidence level]
- [Conclusion 2...]

## What we shipped / did

- [Specific thing: feature, outreach, conversation, decision]
- [Another thing...]

## What we heard from users / customers

> "[Direct quote from a user — verbatim if possible]" — [context]

**Signal summary:** [What the conversations and behavior revealed this week]

## The surprise

[The one thing that happened this week that was not expected, and what it might mean]

## The most important unknown

[The question that, if answered, would most change what we do next]

**How to answer it:** [Specific action to take this week]

## The one thing for next week

**[Single most important action — specific, owned by a person, with a due date]**

*Why this and not something else:* [One sentence on why this is the priority]

## Founder state

**Energy:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Clarity:** [Clear / Uncertain / Confused]
**One sentence:** [How the founder actually feels about where things are]

---

*Previous week's one thing:* [If it was set last week — did we do it? Why / why not?]
```

## How to use the weekly review

**Do it every week, even when it is short.** A 10-minute weekly review is worth
more than a 2-hour quarterly retrospective. Consistency is the point.

**Write it, do not just think it.** The act of writing forces precision. Thoughts
are vague. Sentences are specific. Specific things can be acted on.

**Share it.** Send the review to your co-founder, an advisor, or a small group of
trusted people. Being accountable for "the one thing" makes it 3x more likely to
happen.

**Look for the pattern across 4 weeks.** If the same unknown appears in three
consecutive weekly reviews, that is the thing you are avoiding. Address it directly.

## When to loop back

The weekly review is also where you decide whether to stay in the current phase or
loop back. Common signals:

- **Weak PMF signals for 3+ weeks** → Return to customer discovery
- **Prototype does not prove the thesis** → Return to strategy
- **First user outreach not working** → Return to the product (wrong audience or wrong product)
- **Growth experiments all failing** → Return to PMF signal review

The review is not just a summary. It is a decision-making tool.

## What the review is NOT

- A status report for investors (different format, different audience)
- A list of everything you did (that is a task list, not a review)
- A motivational pep talk (it should be honest, including when things are not going well)
