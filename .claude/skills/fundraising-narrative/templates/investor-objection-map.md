## Common objections by company type

### For any early-stage company

| Objection | What they are really asking | The honest answer |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| "The market is too small" | "What's the realistic ceiling here?" | |
| "Why won't [incumbent] do this?" | "Is there a strategic moat?" | |
| "Why are you the right team?" | "Have you done hard things before?" | |
| "What's your go-to-market?" | "How do you get from 25 to 2,500 users?" | |
| "What are your unit economics?" | "Does this business make money at scale?" | |

### For AI-powered companies

| Objection | What they are really asking | The honest answer |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| "Won't OpenAI / Anthropic / Google build this?" | "Is there a moat beyond the model?" | |
| "How do you handle hallucinations?" | "Can I trust the output?" | |
| "What happens when the underlying model improves?" | "Are you dependent on the model?" | |
| "What's your data advantage?" | "Why can't someone clone this?" | |

### For B2B SaaS

| Objection | What they are really asking | The honest answer |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| "Enterprise sales cycles are long" | "How long to revenue?" | |
| "How do you get into a company?" | "Who is the champion and who is the buyer?" | |
| "What does churn look like?" | "Is the product sticky?" | |

## Preparation notes

For each objection in the map:
1. Know the honest answer (investors respect honesty more than spin)
2. Have a specific example or data point to anchor the answer
3. Know where your answer is weak — do not pretend it is not

The best founders treat the Q&A as a conversation, not a cross-examination.
An investor who asks hard questions and gets honest, thoughtful answers is
more likely to invest than one who gets deflected.
