# Growth Experiment Card Template

Use this to define an experiment before running it. Having a clear card before
you start prevents the most common mistake: changing the hypothesis after seeing
the results.

---

## Experiment: [Name]

**Date started:** [Date]
**Date to evaluate:** [Date — minimum 5 days, ideally 2 weeks]
**Owner:** [Who is running this]

---

## The hypothesis

If we [specific action], then [specific metric] will [direction] by at least [amount]
within [time period], because [reason].

Example:
> "If we post in 3 founder Slack groups with a specific problem framing (not a product
> pitch), then 5+ people will DM us to try the product within 7 days, because founders
> in those groups respond to peers describing their own problem."

---

## The primary metric

[One metric. Not two. One.]

**Metric:** [What you will measure]
**Current baseline:** [What it is today]
**Target:** [What constitutes "it worked"]
**How you will measure it:** [Exactly how you will track it — no ambiguity]

---

## What you will do

Step 1: [Specific action]
Step 2: [Specific action]
Step 3: [Specific action]

## What "it worked" looks like

[Describe the specific outcome that would confirm the hypothesis]

## What "it failed" looks like

[Describe the specific outcome that would reject the hypothesis]

## What "it is inconclusive" looks like

[Describe the case where you cannot draw a conclusion — and what you would do then]
